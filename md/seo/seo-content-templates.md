**Intro (Featured Snippet Target — 50 words max):**
A kitchen renovation in Toronto costs between $25,000 and $130,000+ depending on kitchen size, cabinet type, and finishes chosen. The average mid-range kitchen renovation is $45,000–$70,000. Here's the full breakdown of what drives the cost.

**H2: Kitchen Renovation Cost by Project Size**
| Project Size | Budget Range | What's Typical |
|---|---|---|
| Small kitchen (under 150 sq ft) | $25,000–$45,000 | Stock cabinets, laminate counters, basic fixtures |
| Medium kitchen (150–250 sq ft) | $45,000–$75,000 | Semi-custom cabinets, quartz counters, new flooring |
| Large / Open Concept | $75,000–$130,000+ | Custom cabinets, premium stone, structural changes |
| Ultra-luxury | $130,000+ | Sub-Zero/Wolf appliances, custom millwork, imported stone |

**H2: Cost Breakdown by Component**
| Component | Budget | Mid-Range | High-End |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cabinets | $5,000–$10,000 | $15,000–$25,000 | $30,000–$60,000+ |
| Countertops | $2,000–$4,000 | $5,000–$10,000 | $12,000–$25,000+ |
| Labour | $8,000–$15,000 | $15,000–$25,000 | $25,000–$45,000 |
| Plumbing | $1,500–$3,000 | $3,000–$6,000 | $6,000–$15,000 |
| Electrical | $1,500–$3,000 | $3,000–$5,000 | $5,000–$10,000 |
| Flooring | $2,000–$4,000 | $4,000–$8,000 | $8,000–$20,000 |
| Backsplash | $500–$1,500 | $1,500–$4,000 | $4,000–$10,000 |
| Appliances | $3,000–$6,000 | $8,000–$15,000 | $20,000–$60,000+ |
| Permits | $500–$1,500 | $500–$1,500 | $500–$2,000 |
| Design fees | $0–$2,000 | $2,000–$5,000 | $5,000–$15,000 |

**H2: What Factors Affect the Cost Most?**

**H3: 1. Cabinet Type (Biggest Cost Driver)**
Cabinets account for 30–50% of total kitchen renovation cost. Stock cabinets (IKEA, Home Depot) are cheapest but limited in size options. Semi-custom offer more flexibility. Custom cabinets built by local millworks are the most expensive but deliver a perfect fit.

**H3: 2. Scope of Work**
Moving walls, relocating the sink, or changing the gas line significantly increases cost due to permits, structural engineering, and trade work.

**H3: 3. Kitchen Size**
More linear feet = more cabinets, more countertop, more flooring. Larger kitchens cost more, but cost per square foot often decreases.

**H3: 4. Material Selection**
The difference between laminate and marble countertops alone can be $15,000+ on a large kitchen.

**H2: How to Get Accurate Quotes**
1. Get 3 quotes minimum — all from RENOMARK certified or HomeStars-verified contractors
2. Ensure all quotes include the same scope (ask for itemized breakdown)
3. Check that labour, materials, permits, and disposal are all included
4. Ask about what's NOT included (appliances, furniture, window treatments)
5. Verify WSIB coverage and $2M liability insurance

## 8. Internal Linking Strategy

### Core Principles
1. Every page links to the primary conversion goal (Contact / Quote form)
2. Every service page links to the relevant cost guide (captures bottom-of-funnel intent)
3. Cost guides link back to all related service pages (they are hubs)
4. Blog posts link to the service page most relevant to the post topic
5. Use descriptive anchor text with keywords: "kitchen renovation cost in Toronto" not "click here"
6. Aim for 3–5 contextual internal links per page minimum
7. Location pages link to the main service page and to each other (creates a location cluster)

### Link Architecture by Site Type

**Renovation / ADU contractor site:**
```
Home (/)
  ├─→ Each service page (main nav + hero links)
  ├─→ Portfolio (trust signal)
  ├─→ Cost guide blog posts (intent capture)
  └─→ Contact (conversion)

Service page (/services/kitchen-renovation)
  ├─→ Cost page for that service
  ├─→ Related service pages ("We also do bathroom renovations")
  ├─→ Portfolio filtered to that service
  └─→ Contact / Quote CTA

Cost guide (/blog/kitchen-renovation-cost-toronto)
  ├─→ Kitchen renovation service page (primary CTA)
  ├─→ Related cost guides (bathroom, basement)
  ├─→ Permit guide if applicable
  └─→ Contact

Location page (/locations/scarborough/kitchen-renovation)
  ├─→ Main kitchen renovation service page
  ├─→ Adjacent location pages (North York, East York)
  ├─→ Portfolio with Scarborough projects
  └─→ Contact

Blog post (general)
  ├─→ 1 primary service page (most relevant)
  ├─→ 1–2 related blog posts (related reading)
  └─→ Contact or cost guide CTA
```

**Real estate agent site:**
```
Home (/)
  ├─→ /buy, /sell (main journeys)
  ├─→ /neighbourhoods (local SEO hub)
  ├─→ /listings (IDX)
  └─→ Contact

Neighbourhood guide (/neighbourhoods/leslieville)
  ├─→ /listings?neighbourhood=leslieville (see active listings)
  ├─→ Adjacent neighbourhood guides
  ├─→ /market-reports (current data)
  └─→ Contact (book a showing / consultation)

Market report (/market-reports/april-2025)
  ├─→ Relevant neighbourhood guides mentioned in report
  ├─→ Previous month's report
  ├─→ /buy or /sell (intent CTA)
  └─→ Contact

Listing page (/listings/[slug])
  ├─→ Neighbourhood guide for that area
  ├─→ /listings?neighbourhood=X (more listings nearby)
  └─→ Contact / Book a showing
```

### Anchor Text Guidelines

| Context | Good Anchor Text | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Linking to kitchen service page | "kitchen renovation in Toronto", "our kitchen renovation services" | "click here", "learn more", "this page" |
| Linking to cost guide | "kitchen renovation cost in Toronto", "see our pricing breakdown" | "see more", "read this" |
| Linking to contact page | "get a free quote", "book a free consultation", "request an estimate" | "contact us" (acceptable but less strong) |
| Linking to portfolio | "see our kitchen renovation portfolio", "view completed projects" | "gallery", "photos" |
| Linking to neighbourhood guide | "Leslieville real estate guide", "homes for sale in Leslieville" | "neighbourhood page" |

### What Not to Do
- Do not create links with the same anchor text pointing to different pages
- Do not link every occurrence of a keyword — once or twice per page is enough
- Do not link back to the current page (self-links add no value)
- Do not link to low-value pages (privacy policy, 404 pages) from high-value contextual copy

## 9. Google Business Profile Optimization Tips

### Universal GBP Optimization (All Industries)

**Category selection — most important signal:**
- Choose the most specific primary category available
- Add 2–4 secondary categories that are genuinely relevant
- Do not stuff unrelated categories — it hurts relevance signals

| Industry | Primary Category | Secondary Categories |
|---|---|---|
| Kitchen/bath renovation | Kitchen Remodeler | General Contractor, Bathroom Remodeler, Home Improvement |
| Garden suite / ADU | General Contractor | Home Builder, Construction Company |
| Real estate agent | Real Estate Agent | Real Estate Agency |
| Interior designer | Interior Designer | Home Goods Store (if showroom) |

**Business description (750 character limit):**
- Lead with your primary keyword and city
- Include 2–3 core services
- Mention trust signals (years in business, certifications, awards)
- End with a call to action
- Do not stuff keywords — write naturally

Example:
> "DJ Custom Reno is a Toronto kitchen and bathroom renovation contractor serving homeowners across the GTA since 2010. We specialize in custom kitchen renovations, bathroom remodels, and basement finishing — all with fixed-price contracts and a 5-year workmanship warranty. RENOMARK certified. Licensed and insured. Free in-home consultations available."

**Photo strategy:**
- Minimum 10 photos at launch; add monthly
- Cover photo: best project hero shot (1080×608px minimum)
- Logo: clean on white background
- Team photos: builds trust, reduces bounce
- Before/after pairs: label in file name (e.g., `kitchen-north-york-before.jpg`)
- Never use stock photos — Google can detect and deprioritize them

**Posts (use weekly):**
- "What's New" → completed project photos with description
- "Offer" → seasonal promotions (Spring Reno Special, Year-End Deal)
- "Event" → home show appearances, open house events
- Posts expire after 7 days (Offers last longer) — post consistently
- Include a photo and a Call to Action button in every post

**Questions & Answers:**
- Pre-populate Q&A with the top 5–8 questions your clients ask
- These appear in your GBP listing and in Google's knowledge panel
- Anyone can ask and answer — monitor weekly and answer promptly
- Your answers rank in GBP and sometimes in featured snippets

**Review response best practices:**
- Respond to every review — positive and negative — within 24 hours
- Positive response: thank by first name, mention the specific project or neighbourhood
  - Example: "Thank you, Sarah! We loved working on your Leslieville kitchen — the quartz waterfall island really did turn out beautifully. Enjoy!"
  - Mentioning the neighbourhood naturally reinforces local SEO signals
- Negative response: acknowledge, apologize, take offline
  - Example: "We're sorry to hear this didn't meet your expectations. Please reach out to us directly at [email] so we can make this right."
  - Never argue publicly — it reflects poorly regardless of who is right

**Service menu:**
- List every service with its own entry — do not lump everything into one
- Add a price range or starting price where possible (increases clicks)
- Write a 2–3 sentence description per service using natural language

**Attributes — check all that apply:**
- "Identifies as Black-owned / Women-owned / Veteran-owned" (if applicable — these filter in Google Maps)
- "Online estimates"
- "Free consultations"
- "By appointment only" vs. "Open during stated hours"
- Payment methods accepted

**Service area:**
- Set specific cities and municipalities — not just a radius
- Add every city you actually serve (Toronto, North York, Scarborough, Etobicoke, Mississauga, etc.)
- This directly affects which "near me" and city-specific searches you appear in
